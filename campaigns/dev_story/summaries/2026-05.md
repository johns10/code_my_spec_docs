# May 2026 (to date): Wiring the Harness for Daylight

The first five days of May were the run-up to a public r/elixir launch, and the work had the shape of a project clearing its throat before speaking. Twenty-six commits between May 1 and May 5 closed the long-running gaps in the requirements graph, refactored the MCP surface around a clearer mental model, and gave the harness its first proper window into its own filesystem.

The month opened on May 1 with two pieces of unfinished business. Container kickoff fan-out was extended so child nodes survived an all-filters-off pass and surface kickoffs reached every dependent, and a spex_boundary_ready gate was added in front of the BDD chain -- a checker that confirms the project's spex framework is installed and the bridge file present before any boundary-shaped work begins. The accompanying knowledge base for spex was split out of one 614-line monolith into seven focused files covering boundaries, environment, philosophy, recording cassettes, shared givens, and the writing process itself. The same day saw a quieter but consequential fix: criteria kind and rule_id finally synced through the stories API, and the Three Amigos gate was relaxed to match.

May 3 was the longest day of the month. Story 561's orphan-context filter on next_actionable went in, was extended to the full subtree, was reverted when fixture damage outweighed the win, and then landed in a narrower form that filtered untethered components without breaking the satisfaction fixtures. In parallel, the requirements graph was made viable for large projects -- topology, projector, and the LiveView all gained the cuts and shaping needed to render without choking. The architecture flow received orphan detection and patch-mode tools so an architecture pass could touch a single component without redesigning the world around it. The Stories MCP was routed through the local server with working-directory scope, the first move in a larger refactor. Outside the harness itself, an S3 plus Cloudflare image CDN landed with WebP heroes, a legacy URL redirect plug caught content migrations, markdown footnotes were enabled, and 28 blog posts were backfilled with hero and OG images -- the marketing surface getting its house in order before the launch post drops.

May 4 was the speed pass. The execute_proposal path was rewritten to bulk-link stories and write specs in parallel, and a new stories controller exposed the bulk endpoint over HTTP for the remote client. Spex criterion IDs were renumbered to clean up the gaps left by recent story cleanup.

May 5 was the architectural one. The Stories MCP server was retired and its tools moved into a new Product Manager server -- a renaming that captured what the surface had actually become, an agent-facing product manager with story interview, three amigos, and issue triage workflows. A BadMapError in Stories ownership checks for local scopes was tracked down and fixed. The Sync LiveView was deleted and its functionality merged into a new Files projection viewer (story 687), a 442-line LiveView that exposes every projected column, filters in isolation, paginates with stable ordering, and shows ownership against parent story and component. Story 127 followed immediately: a fingerprint field was added to the Files projection, with a migration, eager fingerprinting that leaves mtime stale, and out-of-band consumers that skip unchanged files. The spex set for both stories was written in the same sitting -- thirteen specs covering reaping, validity, alignment errors, parser errors, and projection key checks. A graph view ?preload=true mode arrived alongside, giving the test suite synchronous rendering without the LiveView dance.

By the evening of May 5 the Files projection was visible, the harness could see what it had written to disk, story management had a name that fit, and the requirements graph behaved on real-sized projects. The launch post is a Thursday away.
