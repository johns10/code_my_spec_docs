# December 2025: The CLI Becomes the Workbench

December was a shorter month and a more focused one. The work split cleanly into three arcs: making the CLI a real working environment, refactoring the document and rules layer, and adding quality checks the harness could enforce on its own output. Forty-seven commits, most of them landing in two clusters around December 5 and December 14-26.

The month opened with a tmux adapter. December 5 brought an Environments.CLI module, an Environments.Environment struct, and a tmux_adapter with 158 lines of pane and window manipulation -- the foundation for letting sessions run their work in real terminal panes the user could see. By December 14 that foundation was load-bearing: a single 2,900-line commit ("f7ucking claude works in the tui!!") wired Claude Code itself into the CLI as an executor. It introduced a Sessions.Executor, a tmux_manager, a terminal_panes module, sessions and submit_result commands, and a sessions screen with 351 lines of orchestration. The CLI could now spawn a tmux pane, hand it to Claude with the right environment and prompt, and capture the result back into the session. Subsequent commits filled in the rough edges: env var piping, external conversation id handling, mouse support, terminal switching, pane closing and window killing, and proper cleanup when an interaction ends.

The second arc was the document and session-event refactor. On December 15 session_events became interaction_events, with a proper interactions relational table, an interactions_repository, and an interaction_registry. A few days later the documents layer got its overhaul: the bespoke context_design and context_design_parser modules were deleted and replaced with composable parsers (component_parser, dependency_parser, field_parser, function_parser) feeding through a generic markdown_parser. Specs were subsumed into Documents. The Rules context was gutted -- the rule schema, repository, seeder, LiveViews, and seed task all came out, replaced by a smaller Rules module that read directly from disk. context_design_sessions were renamed to context_spec_sessions, and validate_design and revise_design followed suit. A document_validity_checker was added so the harness could refuse to proceed when a generated design didn't parse.

The final arc was quality. December 17 added a remote stories client and the controllers behind it, letting the CLI manipulate stories on the server through HTTP. December 21 saw "Schema correctness requirement works" -- the document_validity_checker now ran as a real requirement gate. December 26 closed the month with spec-to-test alignment: an Elixir AST module that could read a generated test file and verify it actually exercised the functions named in the spec, plus a Quality.SpecTestAlignment checker and Quality.Result struct. By the end of the month the harness had its own opinion about whether a session's output was good, expressed as code, runnable on demand. The workbench was complete.
