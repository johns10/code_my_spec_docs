# CRO Task 10: Methodology Page CTA and Conversion Path

**Priority:** Medium
**Area:** Methodology page — conversion
**Status:** Todo

## Problem

The methodology page is the deepest-read content on the site (7+ minute avg sessions, 77-minute session from your best prospect). It explains the six-phase process thoroughly. But based on the full-page screenshot, the conversion path at the bottom needs strengthening.

## What to Change

### Bottom CTA section

The methodology page should end with a clear "what to do next" section. Based on the messaging doc, the CTA hierarchy for P1 (senior Phoenix engineer) should be:

1. **Primary:** "Install the plugin" — with the same install commands from the onboarding page
2. **Secondary:** "Get started free" — link to `/users/register`
3. **Tertiary:** "Read the case study" — link to MetricFlow case study (when it's live)

### Inline CTAs

For a page this long, add a mid-page CTA after the "The Proof" section (after MetricFlow stats). This is where conviction peaks — they've understood the method AND seen the results. Don't make them scroll to the bottom.

```
Ready to try it?
[Install the plugin]    [Read the case study]
```

### Sticky or floating CTA (test this)

Consider a subtle sticky bar at the bottom of the viewport that appears after scrolling past the first section:

```
[Get started free]    or    [Install the plugin]
```

Only show after scroll depth > 30%. Don't be aggressive — this audience hates popups. A small, dismissible bar is fine.

## Why This Matters

The methodology page is your best conversion asset. People who read it are pre-sold. But if the CTA is only at the very bottom and they drop off at 80% scroll, you lose them. Meeting them where conviction peaks is the fix.
